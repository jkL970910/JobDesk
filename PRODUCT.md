# Product

## Register

product

## Users

JobDesk serves active professional job seekers who need to turn scattered career material into evidence-backed application assets. The primary user is a 2-10 year professional preparing resumes, job-specific applications, and interviews while protecting sensitive company or personal details. A secondary user is an early-career candidate who needs help translating internships, academic work, side projects, or leadership experience into credible hiring language.

Users are usually in a focused, high-stakes workflow: importing source material, reviewing evidence, creating or refreshing a resume, evaluating a target job, preparing interview material, or tracking an application. They need the product to feel calm, reliable, and explicit about what is ready, what needs review, and what cannot be used yet.

## Product Purpose

JobDesk is a career operating system for preparing, applying, and tracking job opportunities with grounded personal evidence. It builds a reusable Evidence Library from resumes, work notes, project material, guided answers, and review feedback, then uses that library to produce derived artifacts such as main resumes, tailored resumes, interview prep, positioning reports, and application records.

Success means the user can move from raw career material to application-ready output without losing provenance, exposing sensitive internal details, or accepting unsupported claims. The product should make the next useful action clear while keeping the user in control of evidence approval, public-safe wording, exports, and job-specific decisions.

## Brand Personality

Precise, trustworthy, career-serious.

JobDesk should feel like a careful career workspace: structured enough for repeated use, polished enough to trust with high-stakes material, and restrained enough that the user's work and decisions stay central.

## Anti-references

JobDesk should not feel like a generic AI resume chatbot, a flashy SaaS landing page, a gamified productivity app, or a dense engineering console. It should not expose internal AI, provider, retry, schema, workflow-run, or debug concepts as primary user-facing language.

Avoid interfaces that over-index on marketing spectacle, decorative metrics, novelty animations, opaque automation, or raw technical diagnostics. AI should appear through clear actions, reviewable drafts, grounded explanations, and confidence-aware guidance, not through free-form magic.

## Design Principles

1. Evidence before output: every resume claim, interview answer, and positioning recommendation should trace back to reviewed material or an explicit user decision.
2. Show workflow state clearly: users should always understand what is source material, what is reusable evidence, what is resume-ready, and what still needs action.
3. Keep the user in control: sensitive edits, public-safe wording, evidence approval, exports, and derived documents require visible review paths.
4. Use product density with restraint: surfaces should support scanning, comparison, filtering, and repeated action without becoming an engineering console.
5. Prefer earned trust over spectacle: visual polish should reinforce reliability, provenance, and decision quality rather than draw attention to itself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline. Maintain keyboard-accessible workflows, visible focus states, sufficient contrast for body text and controls, readable dense layouts, and reduced-motion support. Product copy should be direct and user-facing, with internal implementation language kept out of normal task surfaces.
